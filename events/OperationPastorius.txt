#################################################################################################
#
# Operation Pastorius: German sabotage of the United States Industrial Base.
#
# Historically, Operation Pastorius was a failed German plan for sabotage inside the United States
# during World War II. The operation was staged in June 1942 and was to be directed against
# strategic American economic targets.  However, here, it does have a chance of succeeding, based
# upon what decisions the USA player has made.
#
# Their mission was to stage sabotage attacks on American economic targets:
#
# - hydroelectric plants at Niagara Falls
# - the Aluminum Company of America's (ALCOA) plants in Illinois, Tennessee, and New York
# - locks on the Ohio River near Louisville, Kentucky
# - the Horseshoe Curve, a crucial railroad pass near Altoona, Pennsylvania
# - the Pennsylvania Railroad's repair shops at Altoona
# - a cryolite plant in Philadelphia
# - Hell Gate Bridge in New York
# - Pennsylvania Station in Newark, New Jersey
#
# If GER invests in the project, there is a chance that these things can actually happen to the
# USA.  The level of funding by the Germans will affect the "spread" of these events.  With
# increased funding, the sabotage events will happen closer together date-wise, resulting in a
# more dramatic industrial effect.
#
# The USA can defend against this sabotage by investing in the Installation Security technology.
#
#################################################################################################

#################################################################################################
# First, GER decides if they want to invest in the project at all
#################################################################################################
